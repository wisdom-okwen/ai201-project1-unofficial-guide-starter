University: University of Texas at Austin
Note: Written in 2019 (pre-pandemic); some details may be dated.

=== 1. RESOURCES ===

Electronic resources:
- Canvas is the central hub for all classes (syllabi, slides, grades, announcements). Enable notifications.
- The public directory locates professors, students, and staff contact info and offices.
- MyUHS (University Health Services) lets you schedule appointments online and message doctors.
- For picking courses, use the official course schedule page and the UT Syllabi/CV website; check past semesters since some classes follow irregular patterns.
- CATALYST shows grade distributions by professor.
- The Course Instructor Survey database and RateMyProfessor.com offer student feedback on teaching.
- The UT Buy/Sell/Trade/Free Facebook page is good for cheap textbooks and supplies.
- Stay informed via DailyTexanOnline and the "UT LONGmemes for HORNSy Teens" Facebook page.
- HornsLink is the official clubs site (many clubs don't update it; search Facebook for "[UT Austin ___ Club]" or attend org fairs).
- The UT events calendar emails daily listings of meetings, seminars, free food, plays, research, and jobs.
- UTPD timely-notice warnings alert you to dangerous situations on or near campus.
- Access journals free on UT wifi; off-campus, prepend "http://ezproxy.lib.utexas.edu/login?url=" to article URLs or use UT's free VPN.
- The Freshman Research Initiative (FRI) lets freshmen apply for research starting spring.
- Eureka is UT's research database for finding labs; cold-email researchers with a resume.
- UTDirect centralizes registration, Workday hours logging, Bevo Bucks, and degree audits.
- Interactive Degree Audit 2.0 shows graduation progress instantly.

Recreation:
- Kiosks (cylindrical bulletin boards) advertise clubs, events, and research.
- The Union (UNB) has an underground bowling alley, pool tables, and a free movie theater (most Thursdays, often free food/prizes).
- The Big Ticket ($175) gets you into every on-campus sporting event for a year.
- The Bass Pass discounts Broadway/theatrical shows at Bass Concert Hall.
- The Bullock IMAX shows major films with student discounts (no museum admission required).
- All UT museums are free with student ID (Blanton, Harry Ransom Center, Bullock, LBJ Library).
- Walk Speedway or West Mall on weekdays early in the semester to find clubs (free granola bars from Christian orgs).
- UT gyms are free while taking classes; Gregory Gym and the Rec Center have pools, weights, tracks, courts, and climbing walls.
- The Foundry (Fine Arts Library) offers free 3D printers, recording studio, sewing machines, laser/poster printers, and VR studio after training.
- The Maker Space (engineering basement) offers free 3D printing and laser cutting.
- Every dorm has a free piano accessible until midnight (Carothers, Kinsolving, Jester).
- The astronomy department hosts rooftop "Star Parties" on PMA with telescopes, free food, demos, and liquid-nitrogen ice cream.

Free with UT ID:
- Bus passes are free (signal the driver to stop; buses won't halt automatically).
- Museum entry (Blanton, Harry Ransom Center, Bullock, LBJ Library).
- The Sanger Learning Center offers a limited number of free tutoring credits plus drop-in help.
- The University Writing Center (PCL first floor) critiques any writing for free.
- Group therapy is free and unlimited; individual therapy costs money.
- The Orange Bike Project fixes bikes free (if no major parts needed); rentals at the 27th Street Garage.
- Six months of Amazon Student Prime (free 2-day shipping; auto-renews after).
- Laundry in dorms is free.

Libraries:
- Order books online for delivery to any campus library.
- Perry-Castaneda Library (PCL) is open nearly 24/7, has a coffee shop, and houses most checkout materials across five stories. First floor has STEM resources and the Writing Center.
- Battle Hall is architecturally stunning (Architecture Library; no talking/food/drinks).
- The Physics, Math, and Astronomy Library is compact (a favorite north-campus spot).
- The Life Sciences Library (2nd floor of the tower) is the most beautifully decorated.
- The hidden Welch Library (4th floor of Welch Hall, 24th St side) is unmarked and good for group study.
- The EERC Library (new engineering building) has cozy study rooms and whiteboards.
- The Fine Arts Library (near the stadium) has art exhibits and free 3D printers (limited seating).

Other resources:
- SUREWalk (512-232-9255) gives free golf-cart escorts on campus 8pm-2am.
- Free Lyft rides to common student-housing areas late at night (purple zones = pickup, pink = dropoff).
- The Daily Texan is free in orange boxes.
- The Campus Computer Store does device repairs/sales; Microsoft Office is heavily discounted for students.

=== 2. THINGS TO AVOID ===

Crime/safety:
- Always lock your bike frame AND front wheel — front-wheel theft is constant in Austin, even on campus and at gated apartments.
- Check rideshare license plates before getting in.
- Avoid walking alone on Guadalupe/West Campus at night, especially for women; most crime reports occur between Guadalupe and Lamar, west of campus.

Other:
- Jester City Limits charges per dish; use J2 dining hall (one floor up) for the all-you-can-eat buffet instead.
- The UT Co-Op overprices textbooks; buy used from upperclassmen or online.
- Use Dine-In Dollars at dining halls (not credit/cash) for a UT discount of about $3 per meal.
- Avoid food lines via Tapingo (pre-order app).
- Always bring a jacket to large auditoriums — UT aggressively over-cools buildings even in 105-degree heat.

=== 3. FOOD ===

Everyday: As an on-campus freshman you'll mostly eat at Kinsolving and J2 (Jester 2nd floor) buffet dining halls. Kinsolving has more vegan options; J2 has a coveted stir-fry bar but is more crowded. Use Eco-To-Go boxes to take food elsewhere. O's Campus Cafe has higher-quality grill food. Off-campus groceries: HEB (42nd St) is cheap with good bus routes; Central Market for premium; CVS (Guadalupe) for convenience.

Eating out (meals): Kerbey Lane Cafe (Guadalupe, open 24/7, best pricing), Red River Street Cafe (cheaper Kerbey alternative), Don's Japanese Kitchen, Cafe Medici (coffee/study spot), Bouldin Creek Cafe (best vegan), Teji's (Indian), Austin's Pizza (best pizza value), Titaya's and Madam Mam's (Thai), Halal Bros, Kismet Cafe and CAVA (Mediterranean), Einstein's Bagels, Raising Cane's, Cain and Abel's (bar/grill), and the 26th & Rio Grande food trucks (Julie's Noodles is outstanding).

Dessert: Amy's Ice Cream, Tiff's Treats (delivery), Gourdough's Big Fat Donuts.

=== 4. HOUSING ===

Secure housing immediately — UT's "freshman housing guarantee" is effectively false, and good off-campus apartments fill nine months in advance. Sign up for roommate/room selection by email immediately upon a housing offer. Dorms generally don't allow pets (therapy animals OK with documentation). Printing costs money.

Dorm notes:
- Honors Quad (honors students or their roommates only): gated night access, close to classes and Kinsolving. Littlefield (freshman girls only) is the oldest, stunningly decorated, with the smallest rooms.
- Carothers and Andrews have larger rooms and personal sinks (mostly communal bathrooms); they connect to "The Q" basement (TV, pool, computers, printer).
- Kinsolving (all-girls): exercise room, a buffet dining hall, coffee shop, convenience store; unrenovated.
- Duren: expensive, far from classes, exercise room, near Kinsolving.
- Whitis Court: largest rooms, far from classes, tight-knit communities, cold (communal thermostats), communal bathrooms, no sinks.
- Jester East/West: huge, two dining halls and restaurants, renovated, crowded/loud, close to classes/gym.
- San Jac: far from classes, close to athletic facilities.
- Creekside (all-boys): close to Jester dining, SAC, gym, classes.

Off-campus: West Campus apartments are expensive and loud (frats/sororities). Dobie (south campus) is pricey but convenient. The Castilian is an expensive high-rise with its own dining hall and gym. Riverside is affordable but very far and bus-dependent (unreliable transit). Realtor services are free (landlords pay).

=== 5. JOBS ===

UGTA (undergrad teaching assistant), Sanger Learning Center tutor, University Writing Center consultant, Daily Texan (reporter/designer/opinion), UGRA (undergrad research assistant — join science labs starting freshman spring; pay/credit/hours vary by professor; search Eureka), RA (resident adviser — room covered), Orange Bike Project, and paid research-study participation (check kiosks, the SEA psychology building, or the events calendar). Summer research internships: REU, Amgen Scholars, RISE (Germany), SULI.

=== 6. PLACES (VIRTUAL TOUR) ===

- Speedway is the main north-south pedestrian road (Dean Keaton to Blanton), connecting the East Mall, gym, Jester, the SAC, and PCL. No shade; watch for scooters/bikes.
- Two student unions: the Union/UNB (west mall — ballrooms, restaurants, bowling) and the SAC (east mall — ballrooms, restaurants, study spaces).
- The Co-Op is overpriced; avoid for textbooks.
- Guadalupe ("the Drag") is the west-campus border street with restaurants, shops, bars, and banks; not safe after midnight.
- PMA (formerly RLM): ten stories; elevators skip floors 5-7; floor 4 is ground level. Coffee shop, library, empty evening classrooms with whiteboards, rooftop star parties.
- Turtle pond (north of the tower) is a memorial to the 1966 tower-shooting victims.
- The EERC is a gorgeous new engineering building with a great cafe, study spots, and maker spaces.

=== 7. AROUND AUSTIN ===

The bats (Congress bridge, emerging 7-9pm nightly — better viewing from the southeast lawn), the Capitol, Mount Bonnell (highest point), Zilker Botanical Gardens, Ladybird Lake trails (kayaking/swimming; bring water), the Saturday Farmers Market, Buffalo Exchange thrift store (Guadalupe), and The Domain mall. Nightlife: 6th Street and 4th Street. Festivals ACL and SXSW happen mid-semester.

=== 8. LOGISTICS / ADMISSIONS / TUITION ===

- Top 6% Rule: Texas high-school grads in the top 6% are automatically admitted, which crowds the university and limits remaining spots.
- Beyond university admission, you apply separately to individual colleges (13 total) and specific majors. "Undergraduate Studies" is one college and can be a trap if you can't get into your intended major.
- Honors programs (Plan II, Dean's Scholars, Polymath Scholars, departmental honors) require pre-application and a tougher curriculum but offer better resources/advisers.
- Costs: tuition is per class and varies by college; summer classes cost more (some students take cheaper credits at Austin Community College).
