Topic: Getting around campus / transportation
University: University of Texas at Austin

=== 1. Shuttle Bus Routes ===
The UT Shuttle System is among the nation's largest university networks, handling over 3 million riders annually across nine routes. Key routes include CP-Crossing Place (#670), LS-Lakeshore (#672), EC-East Campus (#641), and FA-Forty Acres (#640). UT students, faculty, and staff ride free with a valid ID; the general public pays local transit fares. Routes connect residential zones with main campus hubs and integrate with Capital Metro.

=== 2. Parking Permits ===
- R and S permits: popular but often have waitlists.
- C permits: usually available but with limited peak-hour spots.
Permits are purchased through Parking & Transportation Services. Park-and-ride facilities offer budget-friendly commuter options.

=== 3. Bicycling ===
Cost-effective and eco-friendly. Campus rules: 15 mph speed limit, yield to pedestrians, no sidewalk riding, and register your bike with Parking and Transportation Services. The Center for Cycling Education offers safety courses with discounts for UT affiliates.

=== 4. Campus Car Share ===
Zipcar offers hourly/daily rentals; rates include gas, insurance, and a designated parking spot for students with a valid driver's license.

=== 5. Rideshare Services ===
Uber, Lyft, Hitch, and SendaRide provide door-to-door options. Useful for late-night travel and heavy cargo; many offer student discounts.

=== 6. Public Transit ===
- CapMetro: free for UT affiliates showing a valid ID; 14 high-frequency routes with buses every ~15 minutes at peak.
- MetroRail Red Line: connects downtown Austin to northern suburbs with coordinated bus transfers.
- The CapMetro App provides real-time route info.
- Paratransit services accommodate individuals with physical or cognitive limitations.
