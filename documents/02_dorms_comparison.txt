Source: Her Campus (Texas chapter) — "A Comparison of the UT Austin Dorms" (unofficial student publication)
URL: https://www.hercampus.com/school/texas/comparison-ut-austin-dorms/
Topic: Dorm comparison
University: University of Texas at Austin

=== Honors Quad Dorms ===
Located near the Kinsolving dining hall with a central courtyard. Convenient for communications majors but not campus-center.
- Andrews: pool table, theater system, thermostat control, and a piano.
- Blanton: piano and study areas.
- Carothers: computer lab, individual thermostats, piano, and houses the University Honors Center.
- Littlefield: women-only with restricted visitation hours. In-room sinks, study areas per floor, a cafe, individual thermostats, and a TV room.

=== Kinsolving Hall ===
Women-only residence with a strong community atmosphere. Free tutoring most evenings, an exercise room, and what the author calls the best dining hall. Houses two living-learning communities: Women in Engineering and Women in Natural Sciences.

=== Duren Hall ===
Private and connecting bathroom options, study spaces, individual thermostat control, exercise room, and game room. Typically slightly pricier than other dorms (such as Jester). Convenient for engineering majors.

=== Whitis Court ===
Living-learning communities for Sustainability and Global Living. Shared kitchens, living rooms with TVs, and larger rooms. Located on the campus edge with "house vibes" across multiple buildings — convenient to the communications school but far from liberal arts classes.

=== Waller Creek Community Halls ===
Centrally located near Gregory Gym, the stadium, and Jester dining. Ideal for art, liberal arts, and social work students.
- Brackenridge: smallest hall (122 residents), thermostat control, TV room, recreation space.
- Creekside: men-only, computer lab, sand volleyball, recreation area.
- San Jacinto: private bathroom options, amphitheater, study rooms, houses Cypress Bend dining. More expensive than most; known for housing many student athletes.
- Roberts: individual thermostat control and TV room.
- Moore-Hill: individual thermostats, oversized TV lounge, two study rooms, recreation area, Gregory Gym pool view. Strong community; recommended highly by the author.
- Prather: TV room, individual thermostat control, restricted visitation policy.

=== Jester ===
Largest dorms on campus; temperature control is typically unavailable. The author advises not relying on stereotypes about this residence.
- Jester East: private, community, and connecting bathroom options; study areas; computer lab; two dining halls.
- Jester West: two dining halls, Gallery of Texas Cultures, connecting and community bathroom options.
