Source: Society19 — "10 Things I Wish I Knew Before Orientation At UT Austin" (unofficial student lifestyle site)
URL: https://www.society19.com/orientation-at-ut-austin/
Topic: Orientation tips and class registration
University: University of Texas at Austin

1. The dorms are FREEZING. Despite the Texas heat, bring warm clothing — pack an extra blanket or a sweatshirt for cold nights.
2. Your roommate will be your best friend. Jester groups residents by major and floors by college, which naturally brings roommates and collegemates together.
3. Early mornings, late nights. Set a reliable alarm to avoid missing mandatory events; absences can affect your ability to register for classes.
4. There's plenty of free time. Beyond mandatory sessions, you choose which events to attend and have time to explore campus.
5. Attend the non-required events. "The more events you attend, the more friends you'll make" — e.g., the Glow in the Dark Dance Party.
6. It's okay if you can't upgrade your UT EID. Missing the ID upgrade won't prevent class registration, so don't stress over documentation issues.
7. The fastest Wi-Fi is in Jester East. During class registration, this location offers superior connectivity when you need rapid system access.
8. Your advisors are scheduling wizards. They help optimize course selection and answer questions about AP credit transfers.
9. Enter the Unique Number for each class you need in a Word document. Classes fill quickly, so a prepared digital list enables faster registration than handwritten notes.
10. You'll be HYPED to attend in August. After orientation's three days, students feel genuinely excited about starting fall as Longhorns.
