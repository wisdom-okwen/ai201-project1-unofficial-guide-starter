=== Don't Hire "Resume Planning" Services Too Early ===
The author questions the value of independent admissions counselors for younger students, noting application checklists are freely available. "Your money and time is better spent on SAT preparation," since the college list and essays don't become relevant until spring of junior year.

=== Five Key Areas for Younger Students ===
1. Grades matter most — earn the highest possible grades in rigorous courses. UT reviews transcripts at the end of junior year, so early mediocre performance is hard to overcome.
2. Proceed cautiously with accelerated math — rushing through math can have unintended consequences; universities seek completion of calculus-level work rather than premature advancement.
3. Prepare for the SAT/ACT — ideally complete testing and hit target scores before senior year begins.
4. The resume matters for STEM/business applicants — demonstrate subject-specific involvement through clubs like DECA, HOSA, or robotics, not just grades.
5. Summer programs — reputable programs help explore interests, but be wary of programs charging over $5,000/week that merely rent space at prestigious universities while claiming affiliation.

=== College Visits ===
Pre-acceptance campus visits offer limited value for UT applicants because "UT does not offer early decisions and doesn't consider demonstrated interest." Post-acceptance visits are more useful for making an informed enrollment decision.
